Generally, donors must be at least 18 years old, weigh at least 50 kg, and be in good health. Specific eligibility criteria may vary based on local regulations and medical history.

Most healthy donors can donate whole blood every 90 days (about 3 months). This waiting period allows your body to replenish the donated blood cells.

The actual blood donation takes about 8-10 minutes. However, the entire process, including registration, health screening, and refreshments afterward, typically takes about 45-60 minutes.
